# Where do MMO's go from here? And why I think MnM will succumb to the same fate as EQ2

*It's a long title. It's a lot to take in. We have enough data on MnM and MMO's over the past two decades to see the same pattern repeating itself.*

## What's the point?

The reason I am writing this is because I am genuinely concerned for my favorite and personal most anticipated old-school-like MMORPG, *Monsters & Memories*.

I played a stress play test weekend in early 2025 (which was more like an Alpha), and then got into the PvP beta a few days ago. I also have a lot of experience in EQ2. Pre-LU24 EQ2 was essentially like EQ when it came to dangerous world, grouping, travelling being a time commitment, corpse runs, needing other players, etc. It was all there in some capacity. EQ2 shipped with many EQ mechanics toned down.

- death resulted in experience debt, not experience loss
- you kept your items on your body, not left on your corpse
- since it was experience debt and not loss, you could never lose a level

The risk was still there because experience debt means you gained experience at a 50% reduced rate, until that experience debt was paid off. You could pay a portion (most) of the debt back by reclaiming your corpse which appeared as a ghostly apparition in the world with a waypoint to it. There would still be some penalty there as you could not recover 100% of your experience debt. It was something like 90-95%.

I do have experience with TLP EQ so when I talk about EQ I'm talking from what I experienced with the builds I played with back in 2021, and just from what I know is different in live compared to the original 1999 exerience.

## So, why am I concerned?

Both EQ and EQ2 succumbed to the same fate under the hood. The inability to reliably find groups results in people just quitting the game. This was the ultimate demise of EQ2 that led up to LU24 and transformed it into a solo-friendly theme park MMO. It was the reason why I quit at level 42, and never finished off my level 50 grind until months later after guildies started carrying me. They even just brought me to a level 50 raid at like level 45 because, why not? "Can he at least zone in?" Yep. "Let's take him."

The problem isn't the gameplay loop, it's that there are no systems that fill the gap between I just logged in and want to do something to progress, and great I found a group, now it's time to get some real experience and loot. I just could not find groups.

When I dig into my memory archive and really think about what my experience was like in EQ2, it started out amazing. Just like MnM the group play was perfect. You felt tiny in a huge, dangerous world. As soon as you stepped off the docks in Nektulos Forest or The Enchanted Lands, you instantly felt this feel of dread as you walked about 50 yards off the docks and saw your first mob - you press `TAB`, and you look at the target frame. It reads "Heroic", and with `^^^`, meaning it is fully intended for a full group of 6, with a tank, healer, DPS, and support.

It is the most memorable experiences I have *ever* had playing video games.

And now that same fate is already rearing its ugly face in MnM and we're still in closed beta. I have felt this myself, as well as others who have expressed these same concerns in their YouTube videos.

They are taking everything EverQuest did, and putting it on a modern face. Everything you remembered from EQ is there. Anybody who plays it will literally tells you they feel like they're literally reliving 1999 all over again.

Back when I played MnM for the first time last year I asked Shawn about plans to implement a "loot all" button to loot all items from corpses, and a target ring that projects on the ground of your selected target. I was swiftly met with a "no".

My heart sunk right then for the game. And this was in 2025 sometime. But, I was just ecstatic to be able to play, and I really didn't think much of it. A lot of time has passed since then. We've seen play tests come and go, and one thing has surfaced its face time and time again: not being able to find a group.

The old-school MMO formula is great when a new MMO first launches, a new server is opened, or any time there is something going on that brings players together to converge on teaming up ***`and the players who want to partake in these events are able to find a group to do so`***.

## The modern take

Modern MMO's tried to solve this by making the game solo-friendly. Death loses meaning. Your inconvenience becomes the time it takes to run back to your corpse, in most cases 30 on average, no more than 60 seconds even on long runs. Travel is no longer a time sink. Going from Eastern Kingdoms to Kalimdor in WoW was essentially a `Hearthstone` and a short trip to the Harbor away. At most, maybe you'd have to do a quick Griffon/Windwalker ride to your closest City, then hit its version of a boat ride. At any rate, in 10 minutes you can be from anywhere, to literally anywhere.

The reason the last half of that sentence is bold, italicized, and wrapped in a code block to stand out is this is the *one condition* between an **amazing MMO session** and **why am I paying to play a game I literally cannot play**?

The problem is not the intended experience:
- dangerous world
- needing help from others
- death has consequences
- inconveniences are strategically placed to give the user a reason to explore
	- you don't just magically get new abilities, they must be learned
	- not every vendor accepts everything
		- every vendor has a taste for what they'll accept
		- you can't just sell a `worn chainmail tunic` to a vendor that is interested in and sells `fresh meat`
	-  no waypoints, no maps and other quality-of-life perks **
		- you learn where your class trainer is because you learned by looking for landmarks, nearby shops, or simply ask NPC's in the game
		- I believe absolutely no map is a bit over the top, so by no map I am referring to no local map
			- eg: you don't press M to open a map and just stare at the local/interior map
			- instead, you get kind of a region map by pressing `map hotkey` - you see your zone relative to where it is logically to other zones so you can say "okay, to get to the docks it looks like I need to head south"
	- traveling is a commitment
		- I have experienced situations where it has taken me 30 minutes to get to my group
- social aspect, no single player can do everything in the game
	- everything ties back to `needing help from others`
	- this means you'l see someone at the `west gate` in `city` selling SoW buffs
		- non-Bards/Druids all will want this at some point especially if they need to travel
			- ties back to `dangerous world` and `death has consequences`

This is not an exhaustive list. This isn't even a non-exhaustive list. This is just enough to paint the picture *why the formula is so magical*.

It's a solid formula that has brought millions together, friendships made, relationships started, and endless hours of fun as long as you have a well balanced full 6-player party that has a tank, a healer, a support class, and some solid DPS. A solid puller (like a Bard or Ranger) is a plus, but it's no where required to be in the phase of I'm vibing with some people and we're destroying these mobs.

Where old-school MMO's fall apart is the bridge between "I just logged in" and "I found a group" because for many classes any time in between logging in and receiving a group invite means you've not been able to do anything and this is the fundamental problem.
